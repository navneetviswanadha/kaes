Add Arrows

When arrows are added to the kin term map, a single arrow is used regardless of whether or not it begins or ends at a term or at its equivalent term. This corresponds to the KAES property that a term and its equivalent term actually represent a single kin term. The equivalent term is used for clarity in the kin term map. When the equivalent term is made into a distinct term in its own right, arrows that were only drawn to or from a term now need to be added to the node represented by the symbol that replaces the equivalent term symbol, and conversely. The KAES program will do this automatically if the Yes option is selected.
