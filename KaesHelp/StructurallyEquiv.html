Structurally Equiv.

Two kin terms are structurally equivalent if the pattern of arrows to and from one kin term is matched by the pattern of arrows to and from the other kin terms. For example, Mother and Father are structurally equivalent kin terms in the AKT since each arrow to Father, such as Self --> Father, is matched by an arrow to Mother; e.g., Self --> Mother, and similarly for arrows going from Father to a kin term.
