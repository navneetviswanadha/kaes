Modify Classes

Structurally equivalent kin terms are based on the pattern of arrows pointing to and from a kin term. The algorithm takes into consideration the color of the arrow as well as its direction (upward, downwards or sidewards) and whether it is pointing to or from a kin term. An alternative algorithm may be used that ignores the color difference between arrows of the same type, e.g., upward pointing arrows can be dark or light blue. If arrow color has been used, for example, to distinguish the sex of kin term mappings, then the alternative algorithm constructs sets of equivalent kin terms ignoring the sex marking of kin terms.
