Self Reciprocal

If F is the focal element of the algebra, a self reciprocal element, A, is one that satisfies the equation AA = F. For example, in the AKT the focal term is an identity element and the algebraic element, S, that satisfies the spouse criterion (without sex marking) also satisfies the equation SS = I, hence S is self reciprocal. The kin term equivalent for S would be Spouse, the kin term equivalent for I would be Self, and with respect to kin term products, Spouse of Spouse = Self.
