Parent Terms

The KAES program has found that different types of arrows (differing in terms of color or in terms of light versus dark versions of the same color) have been used to map the focal term of the kin term map to each of the two parent terms. The use of two types of arrows suggests that the arrows are implicitly sex marked by virtue of the arrow only showing a mapping from the focal term to a single sex marked kin term.

This constrasts, for example, with the American Kinship Terminology where the kin term map uses a single arrow type to map from the focal term, Self, to the two parent terms, Mother and Father.
